My Work

Timension:

Timension is the name of the project that I am currently working on for mgm technology partners in Đà Nẵng, Việt Nam. Timension is an internal tool that the company is developing to replace antiquated, and numerous systems that they currently use. Such systems include a employee management system, a 25 year old project and tak management system, multiple tools to track employee absences and an HR tool.

To fix this the company wanted to use their A12 framework that they have developed themselves to build an internal application with it's own team that can continually develop and maintain their data and system. The application will allow the nearly 800 employees the ability to track their working hours, the board and HR to generate reports, PMs can track and maintain their projects, employee information can be accessed and updated. It will be the one-stop shop for the company to manage and view everything they could possibly want to within the company, and if there wasn't a way to do something, the Timension team could work on their feature in the next sprint.

My time working with the Timension team has been absolutely amazing, from the company culture, to the camaraderie between my co-workers. I've been able to work on both the Frontend and Backend and deepen my knowledge of Javascript and Java. This internship has also given me the chance to work deeply with React, Redux, TypeScript, Spring Boot, and JPA. Creating an application as complex and necessary as Timension has been a challenge of my ability to write iron-clad code and test it to make sure that what I have done hasn't broken other things as well as being bug free.

Being able to also work and be appreciated as a full member of the team, despite being an intern, and being given proper responsibility to carry my own weight and be expected to deliver has been amazing. It might seem harsh to some being thrown into the pit like that, but the team was very forgiving and at one point the most senior developer on the team said I should slow down cause he was afraid I was simply working, but not fully understanding the system. He was partly right and so I learned to take my time and understand the implications of the code I was writing and to reason with myself whether this implementation was the best now and in the future of the application. To also be involved fully in Scrum meetings and Agile devleopment, moreso than my previous internship was thrilling too. While I understand that there are people out there that do not like this Agile developement, I can certainly see the merits and why it is so popular in the industry currently.

As we near the end of my internship here, the team is working on migration from the other systems and while I have tried my best to be involved with discussions on the migration and provide input where I can. I won't be here when the application goes live, so unfortunately I won't be able to see the project in all it's glory when it does finally enter production.
